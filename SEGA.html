Sega Genesis Console

The Sega Genesis, also known as the Mega Drive, is a 16-bit home video game console developed and sold by Sega. The Genesis was Sega's third console and the successor to the Master System. Sega released the console in 1988 in Japan, followed by North America in 1989 and Europe in 1990. In 1991, Sega released the Sega CD attachment for the Genesis, which added a CD-ROM drive. This allowed for larger games with better graphics and sound compared to cartridges. The Sega Genesis was a popular console in its time and is remembered for its fast-paced action games and iconic mascot, Sonic the Hedgehog.
